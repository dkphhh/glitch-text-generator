## Fullwidth and Halfwidth Concepts

In character encoding and typography, there are two concepts of character width:

- **Halfwidth**: The normal width of standard Latin letters
- **Fullwidth**: Width that is twice that of halfwidth, matching the width of CJK (Chinese, Japanese, Korean) characters

This distinction originates from the typographic needs of East Asian languages. In Chinese, Japanese, and Korean, Chinese characters typically occupy a square space, and when Latin letters and numbers need to match this width, fullwidth characters are used.

## Fullwidth Characters in Unicode

The Unicode standard includes a dedicated fullwidth character block, primarily located in the "Fullwidth and Halfwidth Forms" area. These characters include:

- Fullwidth Latin letters A-Z, a-z
- Fullwidth digits 0-9
- Fullwidth punctuation marks and special characters
- Fullwidth spaces

## East Asian Typography Tradition

The design of fullwidth characters originates from East Asian text typography traditions:

- In CJK texts, Chinese characters occupy square spaces
- To align with Chinese characters, Latin letters need to expand to the same width
- Fullwidth characters ensure neat arrangement of mixed text
- Creates a unified and coordinated visual effect

## Difference from Monospace Characters

Although both emphasize character width, there are fundamental differences:
