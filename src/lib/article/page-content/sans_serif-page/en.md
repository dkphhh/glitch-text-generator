## What is Sans-Serif Font?

The "Sans" in sans-serif comes from French, meaning "without." Therefore, Sans Serif means "without serifs." Compared to traditional serif fonts, sans-serif fonts are characterized by:

- Letter strokes without decorative lines at the ends
- Relatively uniform stroke thickness
- Clean and crisp lines
- Overall modern feel and clarity

## Unicode Mathematical Sans-Serif Characters

The Unicode mathematical alphanumeric symbols block contains a complete set of sans-serif style letters. These characters were originally designed for mathematical formula typesetting, used to distinguish different types of mathematical symbols and variables.

Visual characteristics of sans-serif characters:

- Stroke ends are straight without decoration
- Line thickness is basically consistent
- Glyphs are simple and clear
- Strong modern feel

## Comparison with Serif Fonts

Sans-serif fonts and serif fonts each have their own characteristics:

- **Sans-serif fonts**: Modern, simple, suitable for screen display
- **Serif fonts**: Traditional, elegant, suitable for print reading

In the digital age, sans-serif fonts are widely used due to their excellent performance on screens.
