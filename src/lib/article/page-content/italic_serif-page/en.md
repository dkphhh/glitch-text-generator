## History and Aesthetics of Italics

Italic script originally emerged from handwriting during the Renaissance, with its tilted angle mimicking the natural writing posture of calligraphers. In modern typography, italics are typically used for:

- Emphasizing specific words or phrases
- Marking foreign words and proper nouns
- Distinguishing quoted or reflective content
- Adding elegance to text

## Unicode Mathematical Italic Serif Characters

The mathematical alphanumeric symbols block in Unicode contains a complete set of italic serif letters. These characters were originally designed to represent variables and functions in mathematical formulas, but their unique visual style makes them excellent material for creating text effects.

Italic serif characters combine two important features:

- **Tilted angle**: Letters tilt to the right, creating a sense of flow
- **Serif decoration**: Small lines at letter ends, maintaining formality
